26 A BILL
27 TO BE ENTITLED
28 AN ACT
Page 1
AN ACT
HB52 INTRODUCED
29
30 Relating to sexual abuse of a minor; to revive certain
31 time-barred civil claims for sex offenses committed against a
32 minor for a period of 10 years; to require the Attorney
33 General to conduct a public awareness campaign; to prohibit
34 certain entities from discriminating against an individual who
35 brings a claim under this act; and to specify that the act is
36 contingent on the ratification of a constitutional amendment
37 authorizing the Legislature to revive time-barred rights and
38 remedies relating to sexual abuse of a minor.
39 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
40 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as
41 The Light of Justice Act.
42 Section 2. (a) Notwithstanding Section 6-2-8, Code of
43 Alabama 1975, any civil claim: (i) for damages resulting from
44 an injury arising from a sex offense as described in Section
45 15-20A-5, Code of Alabama 1975; (ii) for which the victim of
46 the sex offense was under 19 years of age at the time the
47 offense occurred; and (iii) which was previously time-barred,
48 is revived and may be commenced within 10 years of the
49 effective date of this act.
50 (b) A claim described in subsection (a) may be brought
51 against both of the following:
52 (1) The individual alleged to have committed the sex
53 offense.
54 (2) Any entity or organization entrusted with the
55 well-being of minors, the officers or employees of which knew
56 or should have known of the sexual abuse which is the subject
Page 2
or should have known of the sexual abuse which is the subject
HB52 INTRODUCED
57 of the claim and failed to take reasonable steps to prevent
58 it. This subdivision includes, but is not limited to,
59 religious institutions, private academic institutions, health
60 care providers, social service agencies, and youth
61 organizations.
62 (c) The Attorney General shall develop and implement a
63 public awareness campaign to inform survivors of sexual abuse
64 of their rights under this act, including the revived time
65 period to bring an action under subsection (a) and resources
66 to assist survivors in pursuing those claims.
67 (d) No employer, institution, or other entity may
68 retaliate against any individual for filing a claim under this
69 act.
70 Section 3. This act shall become effective on January
71 1, 2027, contingent on the ratification of an amendment to the
72 Constitution of Alabama of 2022, proposed in the 2026 Regular
73 Session, authorizing the Legislature to revive rights and
74 remedies relating to sexual abuse of a minor.
