4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Existing law does not provide a mechanism for an
7 parental rights of the child's biological parent,
8 except in cases of stepparent or relative adoptions.
9 This bill would create Kylie's Law and authorize
10 the court to allow an individual to petition for
11 adoption of a child if he or she is a de facto parent
12 and the custodial parent of the child consents to the
13 adoption.
14 This bill would treat such adoptions as
15 stepparent adoptions for purposes of applying the
16 Alabama Minor Adoption Code.
17 This bill would also require an individual to
18 establish that he or she is a de facto parent by
19 presenting clear and convincing evidence of certain
20 factors related to the individual's relationship with
21 the child, such as that the individual engaged in
22 consistent caretaking of the child, held the child out
23 as his or her child, and established a parental
24 relationship with the child.

29 AN ACT
30
31 Relating to adoption; to amend Section 26-10E-26, Code
32 of Alabama 1975, as last amended by Act 2025-407, 2025 Regular
33 Session; to create Kylie's Law; to provide a mechanism for a
34 court to allow an individual claiming to be a de facto parent
35 to petition to adopt a minor as if he or she were a
36 stepparent; to require the custodial parent of the child to
37 voluntarily agree to the adoption; and to establish factors
38 that an individual must satisfy to qualify as a de facto
39 parent for purposes of adoption.
40 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
41 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as
42 Kylie's Law.

113 individual who is not the stepparent and who is a de facto

115 to file a petition for adoption under this chapter. To qualify
116 as a de facto parent, the individual must demonstrate all of
117 the following by clear and convincing evidence:
118 a. The individual resided with the minor as a regular
119 member of the minor's household for a significant time period.
120 b. The individual engaged in consistent caretaking of
121 the minor.
122 c. The individual undertook full and permanent
123 responsibilities of a parent of the minor without expectation
124 of financial compensation.
125 d. The individual held out the minor as his or her
126 child.
127 e. The individual established a bonded and dependent
128 relationship with the minor, parental in nature, and another
129 parent of the minor fostered or supported that relationship.
130 f. Continuing the relationship between the individual
131 and the minor is in the best interest of the minor.
132 (2) A petition allowed under this subsection shall be
133 treated as if the petitioner were a stepparent and the minor
134 were a stepchild."
135 Section 3. This act shall become effective immediately.
